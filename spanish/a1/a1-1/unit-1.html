A1.1 · Unit 1

Saludos

Greetings, alphabet and first sounds

1 Hook / Opener

📷 Suggested image: two people meeting for the first time on a video call — one in Spain, one in China. Warm, modern, friendly.

Before we start — think about this:

  • How do you greet someone in the morning in Chinese? And in the evening?
  • In Spanish, the greeting changes depending on the time of day. Do you know any Spanish greetings already?
  • How do you say "nice to meet you" in English? Spanish has three different ways.

By the end of this unit you can: greet anyone in Spanish at any time of day, introduce yourself, spell your name aloud, and survive your first online Spanish lesson.

3 Grammar in context

Part 1 — Tú vs. Usted (你 vs. 您)

Spanish has two ways to say "you" — and choosing the wrong one can feel rude.

TúUsted
Use withFriends, family, classmates, people your ageBoss, teacher, doctor, older people, strangers
FeelingCasual, warm, friendlyRespectful, formal
Quick testWould you use their first name?Would you say señor/señora?

Real examples:

  • ¿Tú eres Ana? — (asking a classmate)
  • ¿Usted es la profesora Lin? — (addressing a teacher)
  • Wei le dice a Carlos: "¿Cómo estás tú?" — (they are friends)

⚠️ Note (注意): In Latin America, usted is used even more than in Spain — sometimes even with people your own age in formal situations.

Part 2 — Subject pronouns (主语人称代词)

PronounEnglishNote
yoIAlways lowercase in Spanish — unlike English
túyou (informal)The tilde (accent) is essential: tú = you / tu = your
él / ellahe / sheél (pronoun) ≠ el (article "the")
ustedyou (formal)Abbreviated Ud. — uses él/ella verb forms
nosotros/asweNosotras = all female group
vosotros/asyou all (Spain only)Not used in Latin America
ellos/ellastheyEllos = mixed or all male group
ustedesyou allUsed everywhere — formal and informal

💡 Good news: In Spanish you can often drop the pronoun — the verb ending already tells you who is speaking. Soy Ana is more natural than Yo soy Ana. As a beginner, keeping the pronoun is completely fine.

4 Reading or dialogue

📷 Suggested image: two people on a video call, one in a modern apartment, one in a café — smiling, relaxed.

Primera conversación

Wei and Ana meet in an online Spanish course for the first time. Read carefully.

Ana: Hola, buenos días. ¿Cómo te llamas?

Wei: Hola. Me llamo Wei. ¿Y tú?

Ana: Me llamo Ana. Mucho gusto, Wei.

Wei: Igualmente. ¿De dónde eres, Ana?

Ana: Soy de España, de Madrid. ¿Y tú?

Wei: Yo soy de China, de Shanghái. ¡Encantado!

Ana: ¡Encantada! Oye, ¿cómo se escribe tu nombre?

Wei: W - E - I. ¿Y el tuyo?

Ana: A - N - A. ¡Muy fácil!

Wei: (ríe) Sí. Bueno, ¡hasta luego, Ana!

Ana: ¡Hasta luego! ¡Hasta la próxima clase!

Comprehension questions

  1. ¿Cómo se llama el estudiante de China?
  2. ¿De dónde es Ana exactamente?
  3. ¿Por qué Wei dice ¡Encantado! y Ana dice ¡Encantada!?
  4. ¿Cómo se despiden al final?
  5. ¿Qué frase usa Ana para decir "nice to meet you"?

6 Question formation

Most students learn to answer questions. In this class, you also learn to ask them. A conversation only works if both people can ask questions.

7 Real-life task

📷 Suggested image: a phone screen showing a WeChat contact — someone about to start an online call.

Task: Your first Spanish introduction

Imagine you are joining a new online Spanish group class with students from different countries. You need to introduce yourself to the group.

Step 1 — Prepare your introduction: Answer these questions in Spanish:

  • ¿Cómo te llamas?
  • ¿De dónde eres?
  • ¿Cómo estás hoy?

Step 2 — Spell your name: Using the Spanish alphabet, spell your name out loud. Your teacher will write it as they hear it — did they get it right?

Step 3 — Greet and leave: Practice greeting your teacher for three different times of day:

  • 8:00 a.m.
  • 4:00 p.m.
  • 9:30 p.m.

Step 4 — Real life: Open WhatsApp or WeChat and type a greeting message to someone in Spanish right now. Show your teacher what you wrote.

8 Free production

No script. No word bank. Just you, speaking freely using everything from this unit.

Speaking prompts

  1. Introduce yourself fully in Spanish — name, where you are from, how you are today.
  2. How do you greet different people in your life? Your boss? Your friend? Your parent? Would you use tú or usted with each one?
  3. You meet someone Spanish-speaking for the first time at work. What do you say? Act it out from start to finish — greeting, introduction, farewell.
  4. What was the hardest thing in this unit? Can you explain it in English, and then try to say the correct version in Spanish?

💡 Remember: mistakes are part of learning. The goal here is not to be perfect — it is to communicate.

9 Homework

Choose one of the options below. No need to send anything — just do it and we'll talk about it at the start of the next class if you want to share.

Option A — Receptive

Find any short video in Spanish on YouTube — cooking, travel, music, anything you like. Watch 2 minutes. Count how many greetings or farewells you hear. Write them down in your notebook. Bring your list to the next class.

Option B — Productive

Write 5 short sentences about yourself in Spanish using what you learned today. Example: Me llamo Wei. Soy de China. Estoy bien. Write them in your notebook or phone notes. Read them out loud three times before your next class.

Option C — Creative

Write the 5 most useful words or phrases from this unit on sticky notes and put them somewhere you see every day — your mirror, your desk, your fridge. Before the next class, make sure you can say each one out loud without looking.
